StablR's EURR and USDR Depeg After Multisig Key Compromise — Losses Up to $13.5M

What:An attacker exploited a compromised multisig signing key to mint $13.5M in unbacked EURR and USDR tokens, then dumped them on D E X pools. EURR fell to $0.85 and USDR as low as $0.40. Losses are estimated between $2.8M and $10M depending on the metric used.
Source:The Block
So what: The minting-key attack vector is a direct indictment of hot-wallet multisig for stablecoin issuance. If you custody assets for any stablecoin issuer or have signing authority in minting pipelines, this is a design review trigger — M P C with hardware-enforced policy rules should be non-negotiable.

Polymarket Loses ~$700K in Private Key Hack
